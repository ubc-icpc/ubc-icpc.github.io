About

Welcome to the 2020 UBC Programming Contest (UBCPC).

The UBCPC will be a five hour contest that serves as the UBC tryout of 2020, featuring 8 problems and a range of difficulties. The contest is best suited to strong individual programmers or teams. UBC students in division 1 are asked to participate individually. Everyone is welcome to participate.

FAQ

How do I sign up for this contest?
The contest will be hosted on Kattis. Registration will be open to anyone with an account on Kattis.

How do I learn more about competitive programming at UBC?
You can find more info and links to us on social media platforms here. If you are new to all this, please don't be shy and join our discord where you can ask us questions, and participate in problem discussions.

Should I participate in this contest if I'm new to competitive programming?
You are welcome to participate, but you may find the questions challenging. If you are a UBC student, we will be hosting other tryouts and practices for beginners (including one on Oct. 17th) that you can find more information about on our Facebook page and Discord.

What languages are supported?
Whatever languages Kattis supports.

Are contestants allowed reference materials or internet access?
Contestants are permitted to look up documentation for any of the supported languages. You may access any personal code or templates that you have prewritten. Please do not use the internet for anything else.
Remote participants are requested to adhere to these rules as well.

Are contestants allowed to copy/paste pre-written code?
Yes.

Will there be prizes?
You can get the sense of pride and accomplishment for doing well in the contest.

What is this tryout for?
We are forming teams for 2020 Pacific NW ICPC Regional happening sometime in February or March 2021 (probably). We will be forming Div 2 teams consisting of newcomers to competitive programming as well as Div 1 teams consiting of more experienced programmers. Top performing Div 1 teams in our region will qualify for something (either the North American Championship 2021 or ICPC World Finals 2021).

How does team selection work?
We take into account results from this contest, select practices, and Codeforces rating when making teams. We will rotate and test out teams based on the results of these contests before the regionals.
